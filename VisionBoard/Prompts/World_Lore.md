# World Lore — Vision Board

Use this when generating prompts or design for the world’s metaphysics, moral system, and narrative frame. Source: [../Core/VISION.md](../Core/VISION.md), sin/virtue and campaign docs.

---

## Theme

**"Love as Epic Quest"** — Dopamine from intense combat + Oxytocin from nurturing bonds. Players build and protect a life worth fighting for. Roles for casual (healer/home) and hardcore (protector) players.

**By day** we nurture and build (physical world, horses, tools). **By night** our astral selves defend the land (spiritual world, spirit abilities, flight, monsters).

---

## Moral system: Seven Sins & Virtues

Skills, passives, and abilities are tied to the Seven Deadly Sins and their positive counterparts. Each pair is a **spectrum** from **-1** (sin) to **0** (neutral) to **+1** (virtue). Environments and factions in the 7 levels are shaped by these aspects; player choices influence the player’s level on each axis.

| Sin (negative) | Virtue (positive) |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Greed | Generosity |
| Gluttony | Abstinence |
| Sloth | Productivity |
| Wrath | Grace |
| Pride | Humility |
| Envy | Fulfillment |
| Lust | Love |

**In play:** +1 (virtue) = more loot, experience, and life; sustainable. -1 (sin) = strong short-term passives/abilities that become detrimental over time. Virtue = slow, permanent, compounding; sin = fast payoff, then cost.

---

## Physical vs spiritual worlds

- **Day – physical world:** We interact with the physical world: love each other, work on the home, build, cook, play, collect resources, socialize. We ride horses and use tools. We collect **physical goods** (materials, food, supplies). The homestead is the safe place; no death mechanics during the day.
- **Night – spiritual world:** We go to bed and our **astral bodies** defend the land from evil spirits and monsters. We use **spirit abilities**, fly, defeat monsters and spirits. We collect **spiritual artefacts and power**. If we die in astral form we wake up in the morning; restoration happens during the day. **Outside the homestead at night** (in the flesh): an **ancient ghost** hunts the player; death there means resurrection by the partner back at home.

---

## Conversion (strip sin → loved form)

We **do not kill** enemies. Combat **strips them of their "sin"** and **converts them to their "loved" version**. Once converted, former monsters can become **vendors**, **helpers**, **quest givers**, or **join the homestead as pets or workers**. The goal is to redeem, not destroy; aligns with the sin/virtue spectrum.

---

## The 7 levels (sin-themed planetoids)

Each level is a **themed planetoid** aligned with one of the Seven Deadly Sins (Pride, Greed, Wrath, Envy, Gluttony, Lust, Sloth). Levels, factions, and encounters are shaped by that sin and its virtue counterpart. Planetoid generation is distinct per level (surface + layers, per-level PCG); Astroneer-inspired, non-deformable.

---

## Succession and end

- **Final boss:** Player is defeated and dies but the enemy is slain. The player’s **child** takes over, defeats or seals the villain, returns to the homestead, and starts their own family. Succession: the next generation carries on.
- **Endgame:** Finish homestead, style home, raise generations, trade, build a roster of characters with different playstyles. Completion, expression, and dynasty — not a single “win” state.
- **Death (no heirs):** Game over; restart tutorial; optional spirit/ancestry retention.
