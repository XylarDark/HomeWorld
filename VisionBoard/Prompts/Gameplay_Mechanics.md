# Gameplay Mechanics — Vision Board

Use this when generating prompts or design for core loops, combat, day/night, conversion, and roles. Source: [../Core/VISION.md](../Core/VISION.md), [../Core/PROTOTYPE_SCOPE.md](../Core/PROTOTYPE_SCOPE.md), task docs in [docs/TaskLists/TaskSpecs/](../../docs/TaskLists/TaskSpecs/) (CONVERSION_NOT_KILL, DAY_RESTORATION_LOOP, SIN_VIRTUE_SPECTRUM, etc.).

## Core loop (Act 1)

- **Explore → fight → build** (solo, 2–3h). Family taken; lone wanderer secures a base for the rescue.
- **Week 1 playtest:** Survive 3 missions: crash → scout → boss → claim home.
- **MVP:** First playable loop = explore → fight → build. No family or co-op yet.

---

## Day (physical world)

- **Activities:** Working on the home, building, cooking food, playing, collecting resources, socializing. Ride horses, use tools. Collect **physical goods** (materials, food, supplies).
- **Roles by day:** (1) **Support/healer/caretaker** — Cooking and having meals with family; restores what was lost and nurtures bonds. (2) **Explorer/builder** — Collecting resources, building, exploring the planetoid. (3) **Child** — Create your child via a **mini-game** (press a sequence of keys in a song-like pattern; when you match the pattern, the child spawns). Then take care of them (play, keep safe).
- **Goal of the day:** Build up **love** (meals, care, building, child) → **bonuses used during the night** (stronger astral combat, better restoration).
- **Restoration:** No automatic health restore at dawn. Health and losses from astral combat are restored **during the day** through food, care, and wholesome living. Day activities grant **buffs** for the next night.
- **Death during the day:** No mechanics to die during the day; the day is safe. Later: ability to **enter the astral during the day** (progression unlock).

---

## Night (spiritual / astral world)

- **Astral bodies** defend the land from evil spirits and monsters. **Combat** part of the game. Use **spirit abilities**, fly around, defeat monsters and spirits. Collect **spiritual artefacts and power**. How well you spent the day carries into the night as restoration and buffs.
- **Astral death:** If you die in astral form, you return to your body and wake up in the morning. No permanent death from night combat. What was lost is restored **during the day**, not at dawn.
- **Outside homestead at night (physical):** If the player is **outside the homestead when it is night**, an **ancient ghost** hunts them and can kill the player quickly. Homestead = safe place at night. **Late game:** Player can grow strong enough to kill the ancient ghost and survive the night while exploring, but **accrues negative traits** the more they stay up.
- **Death outside at night:** If killed by the ancient ghost, player is **sent back home** and **resurrected by their wife (partner)**. Setback, not permanent death.

---

## Combat (vision; placeholder until full vision board pass)

- **Scope:** Placeholder abilities, UI, and stubs are fine until a full vision board pass on combat. Avoid deep systems (detailed hit reactions, combo trees, weapon variety); keep minimal and replaceable.
- **Conversion, not killing:** Combat **strips foes of their "sin"** and **converts them to their "loved" version**. Converted foes can become **vendors**, **helpers**, **quest givers**, or **homestead pets/workers**.
- **Night encounters:** (1) **Waves at home** — Waves spawn against your home; you and family defend and convert. (2) **Packs on the planetoid** — Monsters spawn across the planetoid; you explore to find and convert packs. (3) **Key interest places** — Bigger monsters and bosses at key points. **Goal:** Limited time each night; split between defending and exploring; progress until you can clear a full planetoid in one night (all foes converted).
- **Combat variety:** **Defend (waves at home):** Defenses around homestead; **ranged attacks** from defenses or **ground AOE**. **Planetoid (away from home):** **Combos** and **single-target damage**. End-game: use either AOE or single-target in either situation.

## Moral system (sin/virtue spectrum)

- **Axes:** Each pair is a spectrum **-1** (sin) to **0** (neutral) to **+1** (virtue). **0** is shared; **-1** and **+1** are commitment.
- **In play:** **+1 (virtue)** tends to give more loot, experience, and life; **-1 (sin)** gives strong short-term passives/abilities that become detrimental over time. Virtue = sustainable; sin = tempting but costly long-term.
- **Design rule:** Bad choices → trait or ability that is beneficial temporarily then **negative side effects**. Good choices → take longer but **permanent** and **stronger over time**.

| Sin (-1) | Virtue (+1) |
|----------|-------------|
| Greed | Generosity |
| Gluttony | Abstinence |
| Sloth | Productivity |
| Wrath | Grace |
| Pride | Humility |
| Envy | Fulfillment |
| Lust | Love |

## Death and succession

- **Death outside homestead at night:** Resurrected by partner; return home. Late game: can kill ancient ghost but accrue negative traits if staying up.
- **Character death (other contexts):** Character becomes a **spirit** and is no longer playable. Player continues as one of their **children** (or another heir).
- **No children:** Game restarts from the tutorial. Some progress may be kept (spirit or ancestry buff system). Lost: homestead progression, family progression, that run’s character progression.
