## Campaign phases (high level)

| Phase | Player state | Goal |
|-------|--------------|------|
| **Opening** | Homestead + family | Tutorial (resources, build, magic, combat) |
| **Inciting incident** | Family taken | Survive, set out to rescue |
| **Levels 1–7** | Solo/party | Get family back; sin-themed planetoids |
| **Level 7 climax** | Family back | True villain revealed, absorbs sins, flies off |
| **Act 2** | Ruined homestead | Repair; day = defend, night = convert (strip sin → loved) |
| **Final boss** | Death, enemy slain | Child succeeds, returns, starts family |
| **Endgame** | Roster + homestead | Build all, style home, generations, trade |
| **Death (no heirs)** | Game over | Restart tutorial; optional spirit/ancestry retention |

## Act 1 focus (Lone Wanderer)

- **Crash-land, scout biomes, fight bosses, claim home.** Solo, 2–3h. Emotional beat: isolation → determination.
- **Week 1 playtest goal:** Survive 3 missions: crash → scout → boss → claim home.
- **Handoff:** After the tutorial ends (family taken), the player enters Act 1 — set out to rescue the family. “Claim home” = claiming a **new base of operations** (the homestead you build in the 30-day schedule), not the lost opening homestead; it’s the foothold from which the player goes to Levels 1–7 to get the family back.

## MVP tutorial loop (one day to inciting incident)

The smallest playable MVP is **one full day and one night** in the homestead, ending when the player wakes to find the family taken.

1. Wake up in homestead (with partner).
2. Optionally create your child via the **child-creation mini-game** (key-sequence, song-like pattern; when you match, the child spawns).
3. Have breakfast (with family).
4. Complete one love task with your partner.
5. Play one game with your child (if created).
6. Collect wood, mine ore, pick flowers.
7. Have lunch.
8. Have dinner.
9. Go to bed.
10. Spectral self — go out into the world.
11. Combat with an encampment.
12. Beat the boss.
13. Night ends.
14. **Wake up — the family is taken.** (End of tutorial; start of main campaign.)

All mechanics in this loop are **simple versions** for the MVP.

## Planetoid flow (MVP)

- **Map shape:** Sphere. Entire area **segmented into biomes** (desert, forest, marsh, canyon). Each biome has: a **harvest place**, a **couple of monster camps**, **one camp outside a dungeon**, and a **side quest giver**.
- **Homestead and glide:** The **homestead starts on a plateau at the top of a mountain**. From there the player can **glide down** to the ground to explore. When you arrive on a planetoid, the homestead is that plateau base; you venture out (including gliding down) to explore, harvest, defend, and convert. **When you complete a planetoid** (clear its challenges, convert its foes), you **move on** — the homestead lifts and travels to the next.
- **MVP scope (planetoid):** The entire MVP is the user **completing day/night cycles while they explore the planetoid**. Core loop: day/night cycles, exploration of the spherical planetoid segmented into biomes, harvest places, monster camps, dungeon camp, and side quest givers; homestead on a plateau with the ability to glide down to the ground.

## Planetoid progression (post-MVP vision)

- **Chunks/biomes:** Different sections of a planetoid host different content (desert, forest, marsh, canyon); stream or load independently.
- **Destroyable vs persistent:** **Only harvestables** (trees, rocks, flowers/herbs, etc.) are destroyable; everything else is persistent.
- **Below ground = dungeon:** Subsurface acts as a **portal to a dungeon map** — entering leads to a separate dungeon level.
- **Unlock loop:** As you **conquer the planetoid and each biome**, you **unlock items**. Those items are used **back home at your spaceship** to **choose which biomes appear on your next planetoid**. This biome-selection unlock loop runs **throughout the game**.

## Pride (first MVP planetoid)

- **Biome focus:** Canyons, valleys, mountains, large spires. Surface area: Astroneer-like (medium) as reference.
- **Canyon** biome is the primary harvestable/dungeon fit; Pride’s overall surface character is canyon–valley–mountain–spire.
